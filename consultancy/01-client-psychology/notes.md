## Core Frameworks / Methods

### Before I speak to anyone analyze the following

- Who am I talking to
- What do they speak
- What does it mean when the speak
- Hence how should I talk to them
- What should I not say, what makes them angry

## Key Principles

## The message skeleton (Questions to ask myself)

1. What
   - What happend
   - The facts
   - No spin
   - No interpretation

2. So What
   - Why it matters to this specific stakeholder
   - This changes per role it is the matrix in action

3. No What
   - What you need from them.
   - Specific ask
   - Action or consensux depends in the quadrant

### FDE is a mindset

- The ability to hold the business and the technical constraint in your head simultaneoulsy, in a live client conversation and make good decisions about the tradeoff in real time.

## My Take

- Lead with the so what not the how
  - "We built a transformer based classification layer" instead say "This will reduce support resolution time by 40%" then offer the how
- The hypothesis-driven engagement
  - "Tell me about your current process" instead say "Our hypothesis is that 60-70% of your tickets are automatable". Enter with a falsifiable hypothesis
- The closed loop standard
  - "Great I will follow up on the things discusses" Instead decide before anyone leaves
    1. Decisions made
    2. Owners named
    3. Next steps with date
    4. what happens if a step is missed

## **Summary**

- Diagnostice pipeline
  - Role: Identify who
  - Fear: Decode what drives them
  - Matrix: State & Strategy
  - What, So What, No What: AI Impact & Self-Assessment
  - Send: Action or Consensus
  - Closed Loop: Decisions Owners
